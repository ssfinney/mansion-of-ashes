# Game Design

## Premise

You wake in a shuttered manor after a blackout in the hours before dawn. Parts of the house still bear the marks of a fire that killed a child twelve years earlier. To escape, you must restore power, open the house, and decide what to do with the truth you uncover about the night of the fire and the caretaker who stayed behind.

## Player Goal

- Regain access to key rooms
- Restore electrical power
- Understand enough of the house's history to interpret the caretaker honestly
- Escape before dawn with either certainty, suspicion, or a compromised story

## Core Loop

1. Explore a room
2. Find an item or clue
3. Unlock another space or interpretation
4. Revisit the manor with new knowledge
5. Resolve the final confrontation at the exit

## Items

- Matches: required to navigate the dark fuse-box space
- Silver key: opens the conservatory
- Diary page: optional emotional evidence that improves the best ending

## Central Puzzle Chain

1. Search the house and collect the matches.
2. Investigate the study to find the ledger and learn the silver key was hidden behind the portrait.
3. Retrieve the silver key from the portrait.
4. Unlock the conservatory and recover the spare fuse.
5. Use matches at the fuse box and restore power.
6. Reach the front exit and confront the caretaker.

This chain is intentionally compact and deterministic. Optional clue gathering changes interpretation and ending quality rather than the base progression requirements.

## Endings

- Escape: leave with only partial understanding; survival takes priority over closure.
- Bitter Truth: uncover the fuller story, recognize the caretaker's guilt without flattening him into a villain, and leave with the strongest understanding.
- House of Lies: press forward with incomplete knowledge or distrust, producing a harsher and more compromised outcome.
